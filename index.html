Why this exists

Money is tight for a lot of Canadians right now. Not just a little tight. Actually tight. People are making real decisions at the grocery store, putting things back, driving to a second store hoping it is cheaper. And through all of it there has been no real tool to help. No way to scan something and know what your neighbours paid for it down the road.

What we asked for

We trusted that someone would build this. A grocery chain. A tech company. The government maybe. Someone who had the data and the reach to actually make price comparison easy for regular people. Nobody did. And if you think about it for a second you understand why. Keeping prices hard to compare is good for business. It was never going to come from the people selling you things.

So we built it

PriceScout is a free barcode scanner that shows you what other Canadians near you actually paid for that product at real stores. Not Amazon. Not some online listing. The Canadian Tire in your city. The No Frills down the road. Prices from people who were just there, for people about to go. The database starts empty and gets better every single time someone reports a price. That is the whole idea. Canadians helping Canadians figure out if they are getting a fair deal before they walk out the door.

Why it is not pre-loaded

You might wonder why it does not already have prices in it. Here is the honest answer. Retailers do not publish what things cost in their stores. They keep that to themselves on purpose. The only way to know what something costs at the store in your neighbourhood is if someone who was just there tells you. That is what this is. Nothing more complicated than that.

How it works
1
Scan any barcode
Groceries, hardware, household stuff, electronics. If it has a barcode you can scan it.
2
See what people near you paid
Real prices from real stores in your area. Not a warehouse in another province. Your city. Your neighbourhood.
3
Report what you paid
Takes ten seconds. Helps every person who scans that same product after you. That is how the database grows.
4
Flag prices that are wrong
See something outdated? Flag it and suggest what it costs now. Three people confirm it and it updates automatically.
What we stand for
No ads
Never. The moment there are ads there is an incentive to show you the wrong thing.
No retailer deals
Nobody pays to be listed here. Nobody pays to rank higher. The data is the data.
Community owned
Every price in here was put there by a regular person. It belongs to all of us.
Free forever
Kept running by people who find it useful and want to help keep it alive. That is the whole business model.

Retailers had every opportunity to give Canadians honest price comparison tools. They chose not to. So we built it ourselves because if they are not going to look out for us then we will look out for each other.
