The problem

Canadians are paying more than ever. Groceries, hardware, electronics — the prices keep climbing and nobody is making it easy to know if you're getting a fair deal. You walk into a store and take whatever number is on the shelf at face value because there's no real alternative.

What we expected

We trusted that retailers would give us the tools to shop smarter. Price transparency, easy comparison, honest information. They didn't. Keeping prices hard to compare is good for business. It was never going to come from them.

What we built instead

PriceScout is built by Canadians, for Canadians. No retailer partnerships. No corporate agenda. Just a simple tool that lets you scan a barcode and see what your neighbours actually paid — at real stores, in real cities, reported by real people. The data belongs to the community. Every price you report makes it more useful for everyone.

How it works
1
Scan any barcode
Point your camera at any product — groceries, electronics, hardware, clothing. If it has a barcode, you can scan it.
2
See what others paid nearby
Community-reported prices from real stores in your area. Sorted cheapest first. Updated by people who were just there.
3
Report what you paid
Just scanned something? Tell us what it cost and where. Takes ten seconds. Helps every shopper who scans that product after you.
4
Keep prices honest
See a price that's outdated? Flag it and suggest the current one. Three community confirmations and it updates automatically.
What we stand for
No ads
Never. Ads create incentives that conflict with honest results.
No retailer deals
We have no financial relationship with any store. Nobody pays to rank higher.
Community owned
The data is reported by the community and belongs to the community.
Free forever
Kept running by donations from people who find it useful. That's it.
